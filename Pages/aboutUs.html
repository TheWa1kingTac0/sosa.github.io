Save Our Sea Animals




Our Mission

Save Our Sea Animals is a non-profit organization. Our mission is to raise awareness about garbage in the oceans and reduce the amount of waste and water pollution to the best of our abilities by using drones to collect litter, which we then reuse or properly dispose of. We target the younger generations, those who will eventually grow up to rule the future. We do this to protect marine life and reduce pollution globally, and to give everyone a healthier life with cleaner water.

The Problem

The Great Pacific Garbage Patch

The Great Pacific Garbage Patch is a collection of marine debris in the North Pacific Ocean. Marine debris is litter that ends up in the ocean, seas, and other large bodies of water. It forms around an area of 20 million square kilometers of water. Most of this is made up of microplastics which are tiny pieces of plastic that are not easily visible to the naked eye. To learn more about the Great Pacific Garbage Patch and its effects on the environment, click here.

Why it Matters

Garbage patches such as the one discussed above can have many detrimental effects on the environment, and can drastically decrease the areas of liveable waters for marine life. Plastic parts in the ocean don't just go away; they break up into mini microplastics that look like a soapy foam from a sattelite's view. These microplastics are dangerous for marine life, as over time, plastic consumption can kill them; other garbage such as plastic rings can get stuck on young marine animals and can deform them as they grow. Garbage islands can also increase the acidity and pollution levels in the ocean, which not only harms marine life, but the environment as well. Having cleaner bodies of water means living a healthier life - who doesn't want that?

The Solution

Save Our Sea Animals effectively reduces the amount of waste in the oceans by using partially biodegradeable, solar powered drones with four propellers that each carry a corner of the net, which then separate to open up the net, then rejoin again once they collect the garbage. The drone is made of 3-D printed parts that are made from the garbage collected, and is solar powered and waterproof with a backup battery, tracking devices, and an emergency flotation device. SOSA uses that same garbage to make some of the merchandise that we sell! We have workers that sort out the collected garbage, saving anything that is salveagable, and properly disposing of the rest. Even though there may be and excessive amount of garbage in the ocean for us alone to clean up, we are sure we can do it with your help! By making sure we spread the word about our cause, we can ensure that future generations become aware of the problems facing the environment today, and can work towards a solution.
